# UNBOUND Interactive Overall Rank Trials Execution Plan

Status: hard execution plan
Date: 2026-05-27
Target: all three reviewer lenses rate the Overall Rank trial system at least 9.0/10.

## Executive Decision

Overall Rank trials should become official interactive challenge formats, not generic PT-test circuits and not loose substitution lists.

The product direction is:

- Fixed rank-gate formats with memorable ritual identity.
- Deterministic equipment-aware official loadouts.
- A guaranteed no-gym path for early and mid ranks, with honest minimum-kit requirements for higher ranks.
- No roulette for official rank progression.
- No mandatory 5k, planche, one-arm handstand, muscle-up, bench-225, sled, or box-jump gate for Overall Rank.
- AI may explain, recap, or coach around the trial, but AI must not invent official rank substitutions.

## Reviewer Verdict

| Reviewer lens | Strict score | Approval condition |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Home/general coach | 8.8/10 | Crosses 9 when the app shows one clean official version and hides substitution complexity. |
| Professional S&C coach | 8.4/10 as a plan, 9.1/10 if built | Requires real evaluator support for formats, station floors, load percent, quality flags, and loadouts. |
| Elite/Olympic lens | 9.1/10 if implemented with validation | Drops below 8 if the names are cool but the protocols are still generic circuits. |

Consensus:

- The idea is strong.
- Exercise diversity must improve through categories, not random exercise soup.
- Repeating push-ups, pull-ups, lunges, and planks every rank makes the system feel cheap.
- Broad capability still requires pull, load, engine, trunk, and lower-body proof across the ladder.
- The user should see one official workout generated from their equipment, not a substitution menu.

## Loadout Model

The app resolves each trial into exactly one visible official loadout.

| Loadout | Required gear | Product promise |
| --- | --- | --- |
| No-Gym Field | Floor, open space, sturdy step/chair, backpack, towel/table row option | Apartment/travel-safe, no commercial gym. |
| Home Kit | Dumbbells, kettlebell, bands, optional pull-up bar | Main consumer path with better variety and load. |
| Gym Hybrid | DB/KB/cable/machines/cardio equipment, sled optional | Premium variety, but sleds and machines are never mandatory for Overall Rank. |

Important constraint:

- True zero-equipment cannot honestly validate high-rank pull and carry standards.
- If the user lacks a safe pull option, the app can prepare them but should not clear Apprentice+ Overall Rank until a row/pull solution is available.

## Movement Categories

Rank trials should reference categories first, then resolve official movements from equipment.

| Category | No-Gym Field | Home Kit | Gym Hybrid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Engine | Walk/run, step test, shuttle walk | Walk/run, bike, row, step | Run, row, bike, stairs, elliptical |
| Lower | Squat, step-up, lateral step-down | Goblet squat, DB step-up | Leg press, DB squat, step-up |

## Rank Ladder

### Novice: Daily 100

Replaces: current Awakening.

Identity: anime-style first oath, simple and shareable.

Format:

- 100 total work units under 14 minutes.
- Clean reps only.
- No pain or form-break flag.

Default official shape:

- 20 lower reps.
- 15 push reps.
- 20 pull/posture reps.
- 20 step/engine reps.
- 25 seconds trunk hold.

Example No-Gym Field:

- 20 bodyweight squats.
- 15 incline push-ups.
- 20 prone swimmers with a 2-second squeeze.
- 20 step-ups.
- 25-second plank.

Primary attributes: Vitality, Control, Mobility.

### Apprentice: Operator Screen

Replaces: current Calibration.

Identity: first real standard screen, not a 14-round pull-up grind.

Format:

- Five-event screen.
- Each event has its own floor.
- User must clear every floor.

Official events:

- 6-minute engine minimum.
- 2-minute lower test.
- 2-minute push test.
- 2-minute pull/row test.
- Carry/core test.

Example floors:

- Engine: 700m run/walk, 850m row, 1.7km bike, or 6-minute step standard.
- Lower: 30 clean lower reps.
- Push: 18 clean push reps.
- Pull: 24 rows or 8 pull-ups.
- Carry/core: 80m loaded carry at 20% bodyweight or 60-second plank.

Primary attributes: Endurance, Control, Power.

### Forged: The Finisher

Replaces: current Forge.

Identity: classic descending finisher.

Format:

- 21-15-9 under 30 minutes.
- Each round starts with an engine buy-in.
- Loaded work appears, but no mandatory kettlebell.

Official shape:

- 300m engine each round.
- Hinge/power: 21/15/9.
- Push: 21/15/9.
- Pull: 7/5/3 or row equivalent.
- Carry: 40m each round.

Example Home Kit:

- 300m row or 90-second bike.
- KB swing or DB RDL.
- Hand-release push-up or DB floor press.
- Pull-up or DB row.
- Suitcase/farmer carry.

Primary attributes: Power, Endurance, Vitality.

### Veteran: Deck of Proof

Replaces: current Reckoning.

Identity: app-driven card draw, earned after the early gates.

Format:

- Fixed 24-card official deck, generated by app.
- The app animates card reveal and tracks each card.
- Not roulette; not random chaos.
- Same official order for a given trial seed/loadout.

Official card distribution:

- 6 Push cards.
- 6 Lower cards.
- 6 Pull cards.
- 4 Engine cards.
- 2 Carry/Core cards.

Example card meanings:

- Push card: 10 hand-release push-ups, DB press, or machine press.
- Lower card: 12 loaded reverse lunges, step-ups, or goblet squats.
- Pull card: 5 pull-ups or 10 hard rows.
- Engine card: 200m shuttle, row, bike equivalent, or step burst.
- Carry/Core card: 60m carry plus 30-second trunk hold.

Cap: 42 minutes.

Primary attributes: Endurance, Control, Explosiveness.

### Master: The Tower

Replaces: current Gauntlet.

Identity: climb floors, clear the tower, then survive the descent.

Format:

- 10 floors under 50 minutes.
- Floors reveal one at a time.
- Each floor has a category target.
- Later floors combine previous stressors.

Official floor map:

1. Engine floor.
2. Lower floor.
3. Push floor.
4. Pull floor.
5. Hinge/power floor.
6. Carry floor.
7. Longer engine floor.
8. Explosive floor.
9. Upper-body blend floor.
10. Carry plus trunk hold boss floor.

Example Home Kit:

- 300m engine.
- 24 loaded step-ups.
- 20 DB press reps.
- 20 DB rows.
- 30 DB RDLs.
- 100m farmer carry.
- 500m engine.
- 20 KB swings or fast step-ups.
- 15 push plus 15 pull reps.
- 100m carry plus 90-second plank.

Primary attributes: Power, Control, Explosiveness, Mobility.

### Vessel: Boss Rush

Replaces: current Ten Hundred.

Identity: one hard block per domain, cinematic and inspectable.

Format:

- Six bosses.
- Each boss has a 6-minute cap or minimum floor.
- Rest up to 90 seconds between bosses.
- User must clear every boss.

Bosses:

1. Engine Boss.
2. Lower Boss.
3. Power Boss.
4. Upper Boss.
5. Control Boss.
6. Carry Boss.

Example Gym Hybrid:

- Engine: 800m row/run or 2km bike.
- Lower: 24 split squats or step-ups per side total.
- Power: 40 hinges/swings.
- Upper: 2 rounds of press plus row.
- Control: lateral step-downs plus side plank.
- Carry: 200m loaded carry.

Primary attributes: Endurance, Power, Control.

### Unbound: Threshold Raid

Replaces: current Threshold.

Identity: repeated hard stages with station floors.

Format:

- 65-minute cap.
- Three stages.
- Every stage must clear its floor.

Official stages:

1. Engine repeats: 3 x 400m equivalent.
2. Strength-endurance raid: four rounds of hinge, push, pull, carry.
3. Recovery-control hold: accumulated trunk and loaded hold work.

Example Home Kit:

- Stage 1: 3 x 400m row/run equivalent.
- Stage 2: 4 rounds of 10 DB RDLs, 8 DB presses, 10 DB rows, 60m carry.
- Stage 3: accumulate 2 minutes plank and 2 minutes suitcase hold.

Primary attributes: Endurance, Power, Vitality.

### Ascendant: Final Exam

Replaces: current Ascension.

Identity: final three-part proof, hard but not niche-gymnastics locked.

Format:

- 75-minute cap.
- Three parts.
- User must pass every part.

Part A: Explosiveness and control.

- Broad jump best of 3 if cleared by readiness screen.
- Low-impact alternative: timed fast step-up or med-ball throw if equipment exists.
- Bear crawl forward/backward control option for no-jump users.

Part B: Capacity.

- 10-12 minute engine test.
- Modality matched to equipment and contraindications.

Part C: Strength-endurance chipper.

- Pull/row volume.
- Push volume.
- Lower loaded volume.
- Carry distance.
- Trunk finish.

Primary attributes: Explosiveness, Endurance, Power, Control.

Evaluator requirements:

- Pass requires every required station/event/floor to clear.
- No aggregate-total shortcut.
- Pain flag fails the relevant station.
- Form-break flag fails the relevant station.
- Missing required equipment fails readiness.
- Load percent standards require bodyweight data or a safe fallback rule.
- Engine equivalencies must be deterministic.
- Deck order must be deterministic and replayable.

## Execution Phases

### Phase 1: Schema And Definition Migration

Build:

- Add format/loadout/category/station types.
- Convert existing `OverallRankTrialDefinition` from movement-only performance standards to structured format standards.
- Preserve current progress store compatibility.
- Mark old IDs as aliases where possible.

Acceptance:

- Existing tests compile.
- Existing progress records still decode.
- Old trial IDs resolve to new definitions.

### Phase 2: Equipment-Aware Resolver

Build:

- Implement `RankTrialLoadoutResolver`.
- Resolve one official version from onboarding equipment and contraindications.
- Add no-gym/home-kit/gym-hybrid fixtures.
- Require pull solution for Apprentice+.

Acceptance:

- No-gym users receive one clean official version for every eligible trial.
- Missing pull solution blocks Apprentice+ with a clear blocker.
- Gym users do not get mandatory sled/box-jump gates.

### Phase 3: Evaluator Rewrite

Build:

- Evaluate by station/floor/card/boss.
- Enforce every floor.
- Store detailed result breakdown.
- Add pain/form-break handling.
- Add deterministic engine equivalency.

Acceptance:

- A user cannot pass by overperforming one station and skipping another.
- Failed station appears in result.
- Unit tests cover pass, fail, equipment block, form flag, and load missing cases.

### Phase 4: Active Trial UI

Build:

- Daily 100 progress.
- Operator Screen event flow.
- Finisher rounds.
- Deck card flip flow.
- Tower floor flow.
- Boss Rush flow.
- Raid/final exam sections.

Acceptance:

- Every button in trial flow is tappable in simulator.
- Deck and Tower feel interactive, not like a static workout list.
- The UI shows only the official selected workout by default.

### Phase 5: Program Prep Integration

Build:

- Trial prep uses the user's next rank trial and bottleneck station.
- Prep workouts train categories without spoiling the full trial.
- Recent-session adjustments account for trial prep fatigue.

Acceptance:

- The next two weeks of programming visibly prepare the weakest station.
- Prep never injects incompatible equipment.
- Year simulation shows no equipment mismatches from trial prep.

### Phase 6: Simulation And Reviewer Gate

Build:

- Extend year simulation to include resolved rank-trial eligibility and trial attempts.
- Add personas for no-gym, home-kit, full-gym, heavier beginner, and advanced calisthenics/gym user.
- Export trial attempts, failed stations, loadouts, and UI-ready summaries.

Acceptance:

- 365-day simulation passes without equipment mismatch.
- Each persona resolves an official trial version.
- All three reviewers rescore at least 9.0/10.
- Any score under 9.0 becomes a blocker with a patch plan.
