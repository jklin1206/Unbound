# Performance Standards — Advanced Unilateral / Skill Movements

Rep-based standards for advanced bodyweight / skill movements, mapped to the 5-level
ladder **Beginner / Novice / Intermediate / Advanced / Elite**. These feed the skill-rank
ladder for movements that strengthlevel.com does not cover (or covers poorly).

> **Honesty note.** strengthlevel.com has crowd-sourced standards for pull-up, chin-up,
> muscle-up, archer push-up, one-arm push-up, and handstand push-up. It does **not** have
> reliable single-set-max standards for OAP/OAC, archer pull-up, wide-grip pull-up,
> pike push-up, pseudo planche push-up, or the strict/ring/bar muscle-up split. Those rows
> are **derived** by placing each skill relative to the strengthlevel anchors below and
> triangulating calisthenics-coaching sources (Steven Low / *Overcoming Gravity*,
> r/bodyweightfitness, Hooper's Beta, StrengthAnywhere, ZOAR/FalseGrips/Marks.fitness on
> muscle-ups). For the hardest feats the floor levels are *progressions* (negatives /
> assisted), not full reps — and the top level is a deliberately low rep count. That is
> stated explicitly per row.

## Calibration anchors (strengthlevel, single-set max @180 lb male)

| Movement | Nov | Int | Adv | Elite |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pull-up | 6 | 13 | 23 | 32 |
| Chin-up | 7 | 13 | 22 | 30 |
| Muscle-up (general) | 2 | 7 | 11 | 17 |
| Archer push-up | 9 | 24 | 44 | 65 |
| One-arm push-up | <1 | <1 | 11 | 26 (Elite 43) |
| Handstand push-up | <1 | <1 | 12 | 28 (Elite 46) |

Note the strengthlevel pattern for *very hard* lifts (OAPU, HSPU): the bottom two tiers are
sub-1 rep — i.e. you're still on progressions. That same shape is applied below to the
hardest pulls.

## Per-movement notes & confidence

**One-arm pull-up / one-arm chin-up (conf: M).** This is the load-bearing honest call.
A clean OAP is *far* harder than a muscle-up — so unlike pull-up (Elite 32) its Elite rep
count is single digits. The bottom tiers are genuinely *not reps*: Beginner = a controlled
5-second one-arm **negative**; Novice = an **assisted** (band/finger/towel) one-arm rep.
Intermediate is the first clean unassisted rep. Steven Low / *OG2* programs new-to-OAC work
at **2–3 reps per set**, swapping to a harder progression once you exceed ~5–6 reps — which
is exactly why I cap Elite at 5: someone doing 5+ clean OAPs is essentially world-class and
would already be loading the movement. OAC sits one notch easier than OAP (supinated grip,
more biceps), mirroring strengthlevel's chin-up > pull-up rep gap. **Floors are progressions,
top is a low rep count — by design.**

**Archer pull-up (conf: M).** Well-bracketed by coaching sources: need ~5–10 strict
pull-ups to start (so Beginner is a single archer or assisted archer), "3×9 with good form"
is the move-on benchmark (≈ my Intermediate–Advanced band), advanced unilateral work is
3–4×5–8/side. Per-side metric. It's a real OAP lead-up but loads the body differently, so
don't treat archer-Elite as OAP-ready.

**Typewriter pull-up (conf: L).** Lowest confidence row. No source gives single-set-max
standards; placement is purely by **progression order** (Hooper's Beta / NinjaWarriorX put it
just above archer, just below the assisted-OAP stages). Rep counts are my estimate, set
slightly below archer at every tier because the sliding bottom position is harder than the
archer's. Treat as a soft ordering, not a measured standard.

**Wide-grip pull-up (conf: M).** strengthlevel lacks it. Coaching consensus: clearly harder
than normal grip (disadvantaged leverage, biceps contribute much less), but no clean
percentage exists. I applied **~70–75% of normal-grip reps** — e.g. normal Elite 32 → wide
~24; normal Int 13 → wide ~9. Reasonable but interpolated; real spread is biomechanics-
dependent (limb/torso ratio).

**Pike push-up (conf: M).** Slots cleanly **between push-up and HSPU** as requested.
Multiple coaching sites converge on beginner 8–12, intermediate 12–20, "4×6 then elevate."
It's a feet-on-floor press, so it's much easier than HSPU (strengthlevel HSPU Int 12) — my
Pike Intermediate 18 / Advanced 30 sit comfortably above the HSPU reps at the same tier,
which is correct (more pike reps than HSPU reps for a given athlete).

**Pseudo planche push-up (conf: M).** Good source convergence on set/rep prescriptions
(3×5–10 start, 4×5–8 advanced, 4–5×8–10 or weighted elite) and the 50–100 standard-push-up
prerequisite. Converted prescriptions to single-set-max by taking ~1.3–1.5× the top set.
Harder than archer push-up at the high end is debatable; I kept PPPU **below** archer push-up
reps (archer is on strengthlevel at Elite 65) since lean depth is the real difficulty knob and
"a PPPU" spans a wide range of lean.

**One-arm push-up (conf: H).** Taken directly from strengthlevel (Int 11 / Adv 26 / Elite 43).
Bottom two tiers are sub-1 → mapped to assisted/incline progressions, matching strengthlevel's
own `<1` notation.

**Muscle-ups — strict bar vs strict ring vs general (conf: M).** strengthlevel's muscle-up
standard (Int 7 / Adv 11 / Elite 17) is **kip-permissive / any-style** and is kept as the
reference row. For the strict variants I applied the well-documented relationships:
- *Strict bar MU* is harder per rep than the general/kippy number → discounted (Elite ~12 vs
  general 17). Per-rep the bar transition is the hardest (catch at chest, more forward lean).
- *Strict ring MU* is **easier per individual rep** than strict bar (shoulder-height catch),
  **but** rings are unstable and can't be kipped the same way, so max-rep capacity is lower:
  the Guinness ring record (14) vs the bar record (25) gives a ~55–65% ratio, which I applied
  across tiers (ring Elite ~9). Note the common caveat: bar↔ring strength transfers poorly, so
  an athlete may rank a tier apart on the two.

Big picture ordering by difficulty (Elite rep ceiling in parens), hardest pull-skill first:
**OAP (5) ≈ OAC (5) < strict ring MU (9) < strict bar MU (12) < archer pull-up (9–10/side) <
general muscle-up (17) < wide pull-up (24) < pull-up (32).**

## Well-sourced vs estimated (quick read)

- **High confidence (direct strengthlevel):** one-arm push-up, general muscle-up.
- **Medium (coaching-source triangulated):** OAP, OAC, archer pull-up, wide-grip pull-up,
  pike push-up, pseudo planche push-up, strict bar MU, strict ring MU.
- **Low (progression-order only, rep counts are guesses):** typewriter pull-up.
