00 — Read this first

The "honest acceleration" layer doesn't exist in the build

VERIFIED IN CODE

The design doc's whole §6 velocity system is unbuilt. No rank-up LV boluses. No skill-difficulty multipliers. No compound velocity (1.25×/1.5×). No comeback boost. Overall Level is awarded by exactly one line: xp += rawAP × novelty. MovementProgressService.swift:476 · nothing else writes LV XP

So rank is almost purely volume-gated. A returning national-level athlete and a true beginner who log the same number of sets reach the same rank. Standing — strength, skill, training age — is nearly invisible to the rank engine. That single fact drives every persona below.

01 — The actual gates

What moves rank — and what doesn't

DRIVES RANK
  • Overall LV — cumulative AP. The dominant brake.
  • Attribute display-score floors — N axes above a threshold (co-gate at the top)
  • Per-movement AP minimums — tiny; cleared in a few sessions
DOES NOT DRIVE RANK
  • How strong you are — intensity sits at 1.0 vs your own baseline; a PR caps at ×1.4 for one set
  • Skill standards — every trial ships skillStandards: []
  • Movement-tier standards — trials check AP totals, not 1.75×BW bench

Per-set AP is nearly flat (~20–28) because the metric is logarithmic. Sets logged × sessions per year is the whole game.

Is this right or wrong? It depends what UNBOUND wants rank to mean. As built, rank is an honesty-of-effort medal — it rewards showing up, not ability. That's actually a defensible, kind design… but it clashes with the anime fantasy, where a returning monster should visibly outrank a newcomer.

09 — So… is reaching Master in year one right?

The verdict, and the fix

VERDICT
  • For a disciplined trainer: yes — Master is rank 6 of 9, and it's volume-gated, so consistency earns it honestly.
  • For the median beginner: it's Veteran, not Master — lower adherence is the real story.
  • The upper three ranks take years — exactly as you intuited, and that's healthy.
IF YOU WANT STANDING TO MATTER
  • Ship the rank-up LV boluses + skill-difficulty multipliers (already specced, just unbuilt)
  • Wire the comeback boost so returners feel their re-earned status
  • Let strength/skill standards actually gate trials, not just AP totals

Closing the unbuilt velocity layer is the single change that would make "different people in different standings" actually diverge — and make the word "Master" feel earned by mastery, not just mileage.

Right now UNBOUND ranks how much you train, not how good you are.

Both are valid philosophies — but only one matches the fantasy on the box. The velocity layer is the bridge between them, and it's sitting in the spec, unbuilt.
